Research staff

Gabriel S. Longo

CONICET Researcher · UNLP Professor (Physics Dept.)

Gabriel’s research involves theoretical and computational modeling of soft materials, with applications to biomaterials, polymers, nanogels, and biointerfaces.

Short CV:

Current positions: Independent Researcher, CONICET; Associate Professor, Department of Physics, UNLP.

Current affiliation: INIFTA (UNLP-CONICET), La Plata, Argentina (since 2013). PhD: Purdue University, USA (2007). Bachelor's degree in Physics: Univ. Nac. Córdoba, Argentina (2002). Postdoctoral research: SISSA and ICTP, Italy (2007–2009); Northwestern University, USA (2009–2013).

Postdocs and PhD students

Néstor Ariel Pérez Chávez

CONICET Postdoc

Ariel’s current postdoctoral research focuses on the computational design of MOFs for VOC adsorption and capture. He uses molecular simulation and data analysis to link pore structure, host-guest interactions, and adsorption performance.

✉ Mail Scholar ORCID GitHub

Marilina Cathcarth

CONICET PhD student

Marilina uses molecular theory and coarse-grained models to understand how surface properties and solution conditions govern protein adsorption on silica nanoparticles, aiming to rationally design coatings with better antifouling and colloidal-stability properties.

✉ Mail Scholar ORCID GitHub

Ana María Cruz González

CONICET PhD student

Ana describes the behavior of polymeric nanogels using theoretical and computational models based on their molecular interactions.

✉ Mail Scholar ORCID GitHub

Agustín E. Salamone

CONICET PhD student (ICB, UNCuyo)

supervised by M. G. Del Pópolo and G. S. Longo (co-supervisor)

Agustin studies the self-assembly of functional nanoparticle superstructures at soft interfaces (e.g., liquid–liquid interfaces) via molecular simulations and computational statistical mechanics.

Pedro G. Ramírez

CONICET Postdoc (ICB, UNCuyo)

supervised by M. G. Del Pópolo and G. S. Longo (co-supervisor)
