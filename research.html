Research lines

Research

Our goal is to understand how molecular interactions and nanoscale organization determine the macroscopic properties of soft materials. To that end, we combine molecular theory, computer simulations and close collaboration with experimental groups.

Line 01

Stimuli-responsive polymers

Polymer brushes and hydrogels that respond to changes in pH, temperature or ionic strength, with applications in sensors and smart surfaces.

Line 02

Nanoparticles for drug delivery

Modeling the interaction between functionalized nanoparticles and biological environments for the rational design of controlled-release systems.

Line 03

Antifouling surfaces

Study of polymer coatings that resist the non-specific adsorption of proteins and microorganisms.

Line 04

Carbon materials for gas capture

Simulation of gas adsorption in nanoporous carbons for environmental and energy-storage applications.

Line 05

Membranes and hybrid materials

Modeling of nanostructured membranes and hybrid organic–inorganic materials for selective transport.
